One of the most significant developments in AI this year is the emergence of agentic AI systems. Unlike traditional AI that waits for user input, agentic AI operates autonomously, making decisions and carrying out tasks with minimal human intervention. These intelligent systems are goal-oriented and can dynamically adapt to real-time data.

Key Prediction: According to Gartner, agentic AI will make at least 15% of daily work decisions by 2028, up from virtually 0% in 2024. This represents a fundamental shift in how businesses leverage artificial intelligence.

How Agentic AI Works

Agentic systems combine multiple AI capabilities‚Äîreasoning, planning, and execution‚Äîto accomplish complex tasks autonomously. Companies like Microsoft are integrating specialized AI assistants into their Copilot suite, designed to enhance user capabilities by connecting to organizational knowledge and data sources. These systems automate repetitive tasks while providing contextual insights to help users make informed decisions.

üé® Multimodal AI: Processing Multiple Data Types

Multimodal AI represents a significant leap forward in AI capabilities. These systems can simultaneously process text, images, audio, and video‚Äîmimicking how humans use all their senses to understand the world. This approach creates richer, more accurate results across diverse applications.

Real-World Applications

In retail environments, multimodal systems combine sales data, shelf images, and consumer behavior videos to optimize product placement strategies. In healthcare, these systems analyze medical images alongside patient records and clinical notes to provide comprehensive diagnostic support. The versatility of multimodal AI opens doors to applications previously impossible with single-modality systems.

Industry Recognition: Multimodal AI and AI trust, risk and security management (TRiSM) are identified by Gartner as dominating the Peak of Inflated Expectations in 2025, indicating mainstream adoption within the next 5 years.

üìä Top 5 GenAI Trends Defining 2025

1. Small Language Models (SLMs) Gaining Ground

While large language models captured initial attention, small language models are proving their value in 2025. SLMs use fewer computational resources, enabling on-premise or private cloud deployment that enhances privacy and security. Forrester predicts SLM integration could surge by 60% this year, as organizations seek cost-effective alternatives without sacrificing performance for specialized tasks.

2. AI Agents and AI-Ready Data

According to Gartner, AI agents and AI-ready data are the two fastest-advancing technologies on the 2025 Hype Cycle for AI. Organizations are investing in architectures and tools that prepare data for AI consumption, recognizing that "garbage in, garbage out" still applies in the age of AI.

3. Reasoning Tools for Transparency

Tools like LangChain are revolutionizing AI transparency by allowing users to understand how AI systems reach their conclusions. Rather than presenting answers as a "black box," reasoning tools clarify the decision-making process‚Äîcrucial for healthcare, finance, and legal applications.

4. Fusion of Predictive and Generative AI

Forward-thinking organizations are combining predictive analytics with generative AI. Predictive models forecast future events using historical data, while generative AI creates content and solutions. Together, they're expected to increase combined use cases from 28% today to 35% by year-end.

5. Self-Training Foundation Models

Self-trained models using self-supervised learning can pre-train themselves on vast data amounts without supervised labeling. This approach significantly accelerates AI adoption by reducing labeling requirements and enabling faster experimentation with custom AI solutions.
